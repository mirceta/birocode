How chat works — and who can drive a turn

A chat in Claude Web is not owned by your browser tab — it's a backend-owned Run. That one fact is what lets your phone disconnect mid-turn, and it's also what lets the autopilot drive an agent: the autopilot is not a separate chat path, it reuses the same machinery you do. These tabs map it out interactively; everything is drawn from the real code (cited at the bottom).

The one idea everything hangs on

A turn runs on a background Task.Run tied to the RunSession's own Cts token — never the HTTP request's RequestAborted. Lock your phone and the SSE attachment drops; the CLI keeps working and billing, and you reattach later via stream?after=N. Because every writer — you, the classifier, loop mode — goes through the same TryBeginRun, the autopilot can never collide with you on an agent.

Who can drive a turn — one slot, three drivers

Every turn must first claim the repo's builder slot via RunSessionService.TryBeginRun(repoId, "builder"). Three different drivers call that same method — so they can never run concurrently on one agent. First caller wins; the rest are turned away. Open the operator gate, then press each driver's button (try two fast) to see the single-flight in action.
