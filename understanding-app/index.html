“I deployed — why am I still seeing the old version?”

How to use the stale-cache fix. Short answer: almost always, just reload.

Three things you can do — try them top-down

  1. 1

    Just reload automatic — no button

    The server now tells your browser never to cache the app shell (index.html + version.json → no-store). So a plain reload — Ctrl+R, or pull-to-refresh on the phone — always lands on the newest build. This covers ~99% of cases. You don't click anything; it just works now.

  2. 2

    Click Reload on the banner when it appears on its own

    If a tab has been open for a while when a new build ships, a small bar slides in: “A new version of Claude Web is available.” Tap its Reload and you're current. (A single-page tab never re-fetches the shell by itself, so this banner is how a long-open tab finds out.) It now does the thorough clear — not a plain reload that could re-serve the stale shell.

  3. 3

    Hit Force refresh Settings ▸ Maintenance

    The nuclear option, for when you still suspect a stale page (a stubborn proxy, a service-worker cache, a weird mobile browser). One button that wipes all Cache Storage, unregisters service workers, and reloads with a cache-bust — a guaranteed clean slate. Right below it, “This tab's build” shows the timestamp you're actually running, so you can confirm you're current.

Good to know

  • You can't break anything. Force refresh only clears caches — no data, no settings, no agent state is touched.
  • Confirm you're current: Settings ▸ Maintenance shows This tab's build; compare it across two tabs if in doubt.
  • Why hashed assets still cache forever: /assets/index-abc123.js is safe to keep for a year — its name changes whenever its content does, so it's never stale.
  • The proxy caveat: the off-box IIS+ARR proxy could still hold a copy; rung 1's no-store plus rung 3's cache-bust are the belt-and-suspenders against it.
