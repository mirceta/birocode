The full tab list & each tab's span (1–4)

Why the buttons only appear with two or more tabs

The pane strip is a sliding window over your tab list, centred on the active tab. It grows outward, spending a slot budget (the pane count) on each tab's weight (its 1–4 span). Three gates decide whether you even see a strip — and they're exactly the "at least two tabs visible" condition the feature rides on:

  • multiPane feature on (Advanced mode) and floor(window.innerWidth / 420) ≥ 2
  • the budget actually fits a neighbour beside the active tab — otherwise lo === hi and it collapses to the single full-width view

Because the buttons live inside the pane bar, and the bar only exists in that multi-pane strip, the gating is automatic: shrink the viewport (or grow one tab until it eats the budget) and the strip collapses, taking the buttons with it. That's the one edge to know — growing a tab far enough removes its own buttons; you shrink it again from the Settings tab.

One span, two front-ends

Both the pane buttons and the Settings stepper read and write the same sparse map (tabWidths: key → 1–4, absent = 1) through the same saveTabWidths → PUT /settings/ui. Change a span here and the Settings stepper shows the same number, and vice versa — there is no second store. The buttons clamp to 1–4 (− off at 1, + off at 4) so the two UIs can never disagree.
