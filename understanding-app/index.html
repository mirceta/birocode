What actually changed

1One new device-local key. claudeweb_dash_panels holds { ideas, autopilot, audit } booleans. Absent or malformed → all false → docks-only. Same read/degrade pattern as every other dashboard view setting.
2Hidden = unmounted. Visibility gates the JSX, so a hidden panel issues zero fetches (Ideas' /api/notes, Autopilot's /api/autopilot poll) and drops out of dragKeys — the free-drag / grid-flow citizen list is computed from visible panels only.
3Layout state survives. Saved positions, the Ideas size/wide/collapsed toggles, and the agents width are untouched by dismissing a panel; a re-summoned panel reapplies them exactly.
4Gates compose with AND. The Autopilot/Audit chips render only when their features (autopilotTab / agenticAudit) are on; a summoned-but-feature-off panel stays hidden. The rail itself rides the already-Advanced-gated dashboard.

What was rejected, and why

A slide-in drawer would have been a second layout system overlaying the very docks the Operator wants to watch. A "Docks | Ops" sub-view switch discards the mixed layouts people already arranged. Toggle chips keep the one existing layout system and just make membership optional. Everything inside the panels — collapse, wide, drag-resize, drag-position — is untouched.

Try it yourself (2 minutes)

  1. Open the dashboard (the grid button in the top bar). Expect: docks only, full width — no Ideas/Autopilot/Audit panel. Three small chips (💡 ⚙ 🛡) sit in the header next to the layout controls, greyed out.
  2. Tap 💡. The Ideas panel appears in its usual place, with all its old controls (collapse ▾, widen ⇥, drag grip). Tap 💡 again — it's gone.
  3. Summon a panel, close the dashboard, reopen it (or reload the page). Your choice is remembered on this device.
  4. If you had dragged panels around before: summon them again — they return to their saved spots. Nothing was reset.

First open after this ships, Ideas will be hidden until you tap its chip once — that single tap is the whole migration.
