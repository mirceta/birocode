The honest answer to your question

1 gap

We have a strong system — for intent. We have no system for "what does it do today."

Across ~12 dimensions we tie or beat OpenSpec on most: review-before-code, single source of truth, harness visibility, lightness. The responsibility you sensed is missing is real and singular — a living, queryable baseline of current behaviour, kept honest as changes land.

What this means for the port

  1. The gap is one cell, not the whole table. OpenSpec's value to us concentrates in the living baseline + change-as-delta. The rest of its machinery duplicates habits we have.
  2. We're stronger where it's weak. Single source of truth and phone visibility are our moat; adopting the tool wholesale puts both at risk until Phase 3 buys them back.
  3. So the real decision was always "fill one cell vs adopt a toolchain." The plan has chosen Path A (adopt) knowingly — this comparison is the evidence base if you want to sanity-check that against just filling the cell.
